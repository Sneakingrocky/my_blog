A short lesson on
Typography:

What is typography?

Typography is one of designs greatest assets. It is the art and technique of choosing and arranging type in order to communicate the intended mood and message to its viewers.

Why is it important?

Above all typography is a means of communication. Poor typography can unintentionally convey the wrong message, mood or even cause stress to its viewer because it is unreadable.

Type Classification

During the 19th century, printers sought out a way to classify and organize their typefaces. Together they were able to separate the typefaces into three different groups - Humanist, Transitional, and Modern. Amazingly, these classifications are still used today by designers working with typography and creating new typefaces. Serif Fonts

A serif is a small decorative line attached to the end of a letter or symbol. Serif fonts are fonts that include this line.

Sans Serif Fonts

The word sans originates from the Middle English saun, sans, from Anglo-French san, sanz, and is a modification of the Latin word sine which means “without”. Therefore, sans serif fonts classify fonts that do not have serifs.
