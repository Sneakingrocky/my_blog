Humanist

About the style

The Humanist types appeared during 15th Century and emulated classical calligraphy and the open forms of the Italian humanist writers. Some basic characteristics of these typefaces are: Relatively small x-height, a sloping crossbar on the lowercase “e”, and little variation between the width of the thin and thick strokes of each character.

Cap-height

The cap height refers to the height of a capital letter above the baseline for a particular typeface.

X-Height

The X-Height is the height of the main body of the lowercase letter (or the height of a lowercase x), excluding its ascenders and descenders. Adobe Garamond Pro has a regular X-Height.

Base-line

The baseline is an invisible line of alignment on which all of the letters sit. Because it is the most stable axis along a line of text it is a great tool for aligning text with images or other text.

Font pairing

Finding the perfect match for Adobe Garamond Pro...

Let’s be honest, when you’re going to be spending a lot of time with someone you want to make sure that your personalities compliment each other well. The same goes for the type in your projects - they are going to be spending a lot of time together and it’d be ideal for them to hit it off. Let’s pretend that choosing a typefaces pair is like trying to set up a friend with someone that’ll have them at “hello”.

The Perfect Match: Nimbus

Why is she the perfect match?

Another great sans serif and serif typeface match made in heaven. This pair offers each other a complimentary contrast as well as uniting characteristics such as similar weights and widths. Another great match for Garamond would he Helvetica because of their similar X-Heights.
