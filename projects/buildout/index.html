About Buildout

B2B SasS product used by top commercial real estate companies (SVN, Avison Young, NAI Global, Caldwell Banker Commercial, Lee & Associates) to power their marketing and brokerage operations.

01. Project Overview

My Role: Product Manager & Designer

Since its founding in 2011, Buildout has worked to help commercial real estate brokerage offices, brokers, and marketing teams create marketing materials that help them bring in more business, build a stronger brand, and save countless hours of manual work.

I joined Buildout at an exciting time. They had just raised 8M in funding and had ambitious hopes to build new products that would be an end to end solution for Commercial Real Estate Brokerages.

I joined as the second UX Designer, and knew that we had our work cut out for us. I ended up wearing the hats of both designer and product manager to ensure that not only was our product solving the needs of our users, but that everyone on the team was aligned with our goals and priorities. I worked with all parts of our team - I rallied our developers behind our product vision, I worked with our support team to ensure they could answer our users’ questions, I managed expectations with business stakeholders, and I spoke directly with our users to really understand how they work.

02. The pressure was on

The vision our small but mighty 3 person product team had been given was very massive:

“An end to end solution for Commercial Real Estate Brokerages”.

Our first mission was to truly understand what that meant for our current and prospective customers.

  1. We started by conducting interviews with different commercial real estate offices (current and prospective Buildout users). We spoke with the teams, Managing directors, marketing members, brokers, accountants, and the swiss army of knives of the company. We learned about each person’s role in the office, what their current process looked like, the tools they were using, what was working well, and pain points they had individually and as a team.
  2. Researched competitors in the space of Contact relationship software, Deal management, and Accounting.
  3. Researched solutions used by different markets.
  4. Studied and dissected the various documents, excel spreadsheets, and data that our interviewees sent over.

03. Outcomes & Lessons

To start, we launched a beta version of our products so we could iterate quickly, test, and validate our work with real data and users. It was exciting to see how much impact our product was making for our customers right from the get-go. People were excited to get in and start adding their data so they could finally have a true view into how their company was doing and how much money they could be expecting at any point. We officially launched to the world in 2020.

Building Elite was a wild ride with a lot of high and low moments. There were interesting challenges we faced because we were unable to unveil our new products to the world yet. Here are a few lessons we learned:

  • Even though new products are very exciting, it is important to remember how your current product enabled you to be successful. At times we put too much effort into the new products, while not continuing to provide value for our current customers.
  • Launching a product is more than just making sure the product is going to be a good user experience and technically stable in the external world. There was a lot of internal team infrastructure that we needed in place before we could properly support our new products.
