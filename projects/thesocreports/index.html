How do you keep a personal touch when automating a manual process?

About TheSocReports

TheSocReports analyzes performance & metrics, compares users to their defined competitors, provides tips, education & suggestions based on activity & inspiration. They empower customers through a blend of personal training and data science to help them succeed in our social media-heavy world.

01. Project Overview

My Role: Project Manager & Lead Designer

When TheSocReports came to 8th Light, they presented us with a few challenges. Their number one pain point was manually having to create a 15 - 30 page content rich pdf report per client every single week. Their hope was to automate this process in order to scale their business and reach new markets. In order to reach new markets, they also wanted to create a marketing website that was representative of their brand and voice. But, perhaps the biggest challenge was maintaining Carol’s voice & personal touch in the platform that she was capturing through the manual process in which she was curating and creating each and every page of her client’s reports. We were ready for the challenge, and excited to work with such passionate clients.

02. The Discovery

In order to dive deeper into the existing product, I led a Discovery session with the client where we all took some time to write out features/thoughts on the current product, and the dreams of what the platform would include using a rendition of the Product Tree exercise. After about 10 minutes of writing our individual ideas down we started mapping out our featurelist/wishlist on a product tree placing needs toward the center of the tree and wants toward the edges. We also made sure to group cards that were similar.

This session was valuable in so many ways. Firstly, it helped build a great foundation for us all to work well together to tackle the tough weeks to come. Secondly, it helped us prioritize and establish what we call “Charters” so we could get organized and have a plan of attack from the start.

03. Research

After the discovery, the team felt we had a clear picture of the client needs but we really wanted to dive into the user needs to make sure we were creating a product that TheSocReport users would both find value in and love.

We began by researching the target markets, social media platforms, and potential competitors. We then mapped out our objectives and hypotheses.

A few of our Objectives:

  • Do clients find value in the current product/pdf reports?
  • What parts of the current product are the most valuable?
  • How are clients using the current reports?
  • What is it like in the day of the life of a media professional?
  • How are realtors using social media?
  • What avenues are realtors finding leads turn into successful sales?

A few of our Hypotheses:

  • Users are finding value in being able to match themselves against their competitors
  • Users do not like scrolling through a 30 page report to find their favorite insights. We can introduce a way for them to easily navigate to the parts they care about most and show the most vital information first.
  • Users are using social media in order to improve their brand presence in their industry.

Methods & Conduct

We knew we had a limited amount of time and budget to conduct research, so we did so efficiently and effectively by interviewing 5 current users of the TheSocReports.

Synthesize

The information we found during the interviews was invaluable in our design decision process, driving the content creation for the site, and helping the team make tough business decisions.
